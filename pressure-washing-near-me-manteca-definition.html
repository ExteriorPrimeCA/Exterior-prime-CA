Differences Between This Topic and Commonly Confused Concepts

Pressure washing is often confused with power washing, soft washing, exterior painting preparation, and general janitorial cleaning. Pressure washing typically refers to cleaning with pressurized water, sometimes combined with cleaning solutions. Power washing is often used interchangeably in casual conversation, but in some industry contexts it may imply heated water. Soft washing uses lower pressure and relies more heavily on cleaning solutions, making it more appropriate for certain painted, delicate, or organic-growth-prone surfaces.

Exterior painting preparation may include pressure washing, but it is not the same service. Painting preparation also includes drying time, scraping, sanding, repairs, caulking, masking, primer selection, and coating application. Pressure washing may be one step in that process, especially when removing dirt or chalky residue before primer. However, pressure washing alone does not complete a painting or coating preparation standard.

General exterior cleaning is also broader than pressure washing. It may include hand cleaning, window-safe rinsing, gutter brightening, roof-safe treatments, light washing, sweeping, debris removal, or low-pressure rinsing. The market standard is to match the method to the material instead of forcing every exterior cleaning need into a high-pressure process.
